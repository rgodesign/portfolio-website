GameShelf

GameShelf is an app that will enable users to keep track of their personal collection of video games across numerous platforms. Users create an account and log in to create a profile. They can add games to their library (called their “shelf”) and mark which are personal favorites.

Process

Research & Ideation

GameShelf was entirely my own concept. I performed a competitor analysis and found that there was no one dominant app for this market. I tested several of these apps to see how they could be improved.

Wireframes

I prefer to start with paper sketches whenever I can, it helps me get my ideas down before I think about all the digital tools at my disposal. Once I went digital with Sketch, I started with iOS wireframes, then Android. Both sets of wireframes were iterated on with feedback from designers and potential users.

Design

The colors and typefaces were chosen in accordance with iOS’ Human Interface Guidelines and Android’s Material design, respectively. Orange, blue, and white were chosen to keep the interface feeling clean and fun.

Prototype

GameShelf was prototyped in InVision and tested by designers and potential users for feedback. Some screens were animated in Flinto as a mockup of what it would look like fully implemented.

Conclusion

GameShelf was received well as a design, with only minor changes made in iteration. Time constraints limited the features, but multiple testers expressed an interest in the implementation of social features in future versions.
